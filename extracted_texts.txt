MGMT2010 Organizational Behavior Assignment  3 :  Personal Reflection on My Experience Working in a Team for Assignment 1 and Assignment 2 . Assignment submitted to: UDST Ahmed  AL-THANI S/N:  60309315 2 4 th   of  March  202 6 Description (What happened?) : In Assignment 1 we were part of a group which was tasked with the analysis of The Coca-Cola Company through the lens of organizational  behavior . The work was not written together, but in distinct blocks of content. The section by Mohammed Al-Kuwari was dedicated to the company overview, mission, and vision, history, and organization. In the section tackling applied organizational  behavior  aspect, values and culture, the leadership effect, divisional structure, teamwork and group dynamics, motivation and change management, Ali addressed it. This indicates that the group started with a division-of- labor  strategy in  which  an individual prepared a particular part after which the pieces could be joined back together into a single submission. The allocation of materials in the later task is even more explicit in terms of questions. Question it is possible to increase motivation in the workplace based on Four-Drive Theory, Rahaf had Question 1 how workplace structure can increase motivation, I had Question 2 how management strategies can decrease stress, Ali had Question 3 and Mohammed had Question 4. My individual segment on stress reduction was  actually composed   of  Four-Drive Theory and Learned Needs Theory with the focus on role awareness, collaborative efforts, growth chances, and appreciation. An obvious due date indicator in the materials is the submission date of 26 March 2026, which suggests that by that time the team was out of doing general company  research  instead doing theory-driven application. Another significant point of turning came when the work was to start answering separately to a coherent group submission. In Assignment 1, it was not to collect  information about Coca-Cola, but rather to tie the descriptive areas to the concepts of organizational  behavior .  Assignment  2 made the task a little more critical since the questions demanded something more than mere description, which includes interpretation. In  this  case, my section did not have the option of defining stress or motivation but demonstrating the practical way in which Coca-Cola could apply management tactics, Four-Drive Theory, and learned needs to help team members avoid work-related stress. During that phase, variations in writing style, level of analysis and evidence usage were more pronounced among the contributions made in the team. Analysis (Why and how did it happen?) : Team effectiveness was increased in case of an explicit task structure. This is true to the model of Tuckman since our work pattern was like we were experiencing movement of forming to storming then to norming as positions became more defined. It also echoes the argument by Hackman that the design conditions of team effectiveness like having a clear task, defined membership and enabling structure are very important factors. In our case, the group was more productive after each member was aware of which section or question to fill. The breakdown in work decreased the feelings of  confusion yet  led to another problem of the members paying too much attention to their own section and not enough to what was being put in the entire work   (TUCKMAN, 1965) . Unlike technical knowledge, the aspect of communication and coordination influenced the outcome. Working in isolation the members were very quick in their work and the entire argument was not always consistent. This could be explained in part by the work of Edmondson on the concept of psychological safety: a group can do better when  members are psychologically safe to question, correct, and challenge one another in their work. Simultaneously, the work by Jehn about intragroup conflict is applicable since a certain level of disagreement can be helpful in case of making the work quality despite getting into a personal conflict. I believe that our team  tapt  into the advantage of task-oriented differences when speaking about the practical use of theory, and when we spoke about the same our team could have been much better, because we could have discussed each section and manage the level of depth, style and the strength of evidence before the last phase of integrating   (Edmondson, 1999; Jehn, 1995) . The information presented in Assignment 2 also contributes to the explanation of the way our teamwork worked. My response on Stress reduction employed both Four drive  Theory  and Learned needs theory and the response of Rahaf on Four-Drive Theory was based on balancing between acquire, bond, comprehend and defend. That implied that we had to succeed based on the ability to match allied ideas among the various answers instead of viewing questions individually. The four-drive model proposed by Lawrence and Nohria as well as the theory of learned needs proposed by McClelland can both indicate that human beings are different in terms of what will motivate them and what will make them feel safe. Practically, the same could be said about our team: there were those who appeared to be the best at  organized  research, the others at the theoretical explanation and the others by organizing significant portions. We should have undervalued those  differences,  and  the end result  would have been likely to be less strong   (Lawrence & Nohria, 2002; McClelland, 1961) . Evaluation (How well did you perform?) : I think  m y points in the field of analysis were high. Question 2 was my primary contribution as I presented ways in which Coca-Cola would decrease the stress of their employees by offering better teamwork, role- clarity , development, and equitable recognition. I was not just defining theories; I also attempted to apply them to a real-life situation of an organization. To illustrate, I have associated stress reduction with the necessity to find a balance between the defensive drive and the need to bond and understand,  and also  applied the concept of various acquired needs, achievement and affiliation. This was indicative of the fact that I had mastered the theory and was capable of applying it to the practice of management, which provided academic relevance to the work of the team   (Edmondson, 1999; Karasek, 1979; McClelland, 1961) . However,   my  coordination and monitoring were not my strong points. I had been very active in producing a dramatic answer to my own assigned question, but less in seeing that the overall group paper was entirely consistent in its tone, citation format and level of analysis. In retrospect, that was a defect. As Hackman has demonstrated about teams, both coordination and individual effort is needed to perform well in a team and as Latané, Williams, and Harkins reveal, groups can become ineffective when responsibility becomes highly fragmented. It was not a case of social loafing on my part as I was taking my section seriously, but it is understandable that I made a lot of assumptions based on the view that people would take care of their sections and that in the end the document would make sense. That was an unproductive assumption   (Hackman, 2002; Seno, 2013) . The other strength was that I used concepts that were directly related to the module material. Four-Drive Theory, learned needs, expectancy theory, and social cognitive  theory have been transparently discussed in the motivation lecture file and I  made reference  to that conceptual base instead of writing vaguely. Nevertheless, I could have done better by reading through more of other sections of the members before submitting it. According to expectancy theory, the more individuals perceive that there is a distinct connection between the effort, performance, and results, the more effort animals put into the task, whereas, according to the social cognitive theory of learning, learning occurs through observation and interaction (Bandura). Reflectively, I need to employ more peer review and discussion, as reviewing the work of other people in a closer manner would have enhanced my section and the overall integration of the team   (Landy & Conte, 2010) . Expression (What did you personally think and feel?) : I think   the team was potentially academic, though I also observed that instead of working together, our team was more effective in its tasks division. I was confident in the process of writing my own section as I was aware of the theories, and I could use them. Concurrently, it was under pressure to a certain degree as I was also looking at the bigger group result since I could observe that I was going to need over four separate answers laid next to one another as a way of creating a good final submission. I realized that I have an instinctive orientation in content  quality and  theory use whereas I am less instinctive in creating process issues including follow-up, alignment, and collective review. This realization played a role in me   (Ryan & Deci, 2000) . And there was one thing I learned about myself as a team member. I am most productive when I have a well-defined intellectual task, and particularly one that involves  interpretation , organization, and use of theory. Nonetheless, I realized I should  be  more  conscious about team facilitation, including, of course, personal input. Preliminary internal deadlines, pre-submission review meeting, and a common standard of one section among all people are all measures that I would implement in future group work. In  general,  this experience made me realize that to be a good team member I not only have to be doing my part well, but  also,  I also need to ensure that I help the team to create a single result.
